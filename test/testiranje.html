Dear Derivians,

2023 is shaping up to be monumental for Deriv! Here's the Talent Management Report for January to July 2023.
Open report summary

1. Demographics
  • Since January 2023, we have experienced an 8.31% increase in our headcount and have welcomed 261 new hires.
  • We have maintained a balanced gender ratio of 51.3% women and 48.7% men.
  • This year, the offices in Malaysia and Dubai had the highest number of new hires, bringing the total headcount to 612 and 240, respectively.

2. Performance management:

Starting in 2023, we revamped our performance assessment framework. This transformation prioritises the alignment of individual goals with Deriv's objectives.

Key changes include:

  • Streamlined the rating scales
  • Revamped and simplified the competencies
  • Implemented an open scoring methodology that eliminates post-evaluation score adjustments and the practice of averaging ratings
  • Enabled skill development evaluation with a focus on 'learning and growth'

Career progresion

As a high-performing organisation, we are committed to providing continuous career growth opportunities for our employees. In 2023, we successfully promoted 115 (8.6% of our headcount) employees to the next level. We have also initiated 32 internal movements enabling horizontal career growth.

3. Culture @ Deriv

At Deriv, we foster a high-performance and agile culture, emphasising inclusivity, and growth.

    5. HRBP initiatives

    Department projects
    1. Focus group discussion: Initiative to enhance Internal Audit team engagement. Valuable insights were shared to understand dynamics and challenges, particularly with 47% of new members. Training, feedback forums, and workload reduction resulted in a notable increase in team eNPS to +29.

    2. All hands session: Initiated quarterly sessions for the Trading team, improving alignment and communication.

    3. Team building initiatives: Hosted inclusive team activities, fostering teamwork and camaraderie. (Trading engineering, Backend, DE & BI)

    4. New hire integration programme: Designed for the Global Partnership team, this structured 3-week programme ensures thorough training and seamless integration

    5. Corporate Governance pulse survey: The survey feedback revealed the need for improvement in work distribution. The TLs reorganised tasks based on the findings. A one-month follow-up showed no reported concerns, indicating a successful resolution.

    Employee coaching and intervention:

    HRBP coaching targets individual challenges for enhanced performance, skill development, and career alignment, with ongoing progress tracking and adjustments as needed.
    1. Career Development coaching (16 employees): Offers guidance on Deriv's promotion matrix and internal mobility for informed career decisions.
    2. Leadership and Performance coaching (20 employees): Custom coaching for managers on skill enhancement, decision-making, and addressing specific challenges through discussions and skill development courses. Individual coaching focuses on HR processes (IDP and PIP) to bridge performance and managerial gaps.
    3. Time Management coaching (2 employees): Coaching to create clear work hour expectations, resulting in notable improvements. It enhances time optimisation, task prioritisation, and overall productivity.

    6. Living Deriv Values workshop

    The workshops are focused on reinforcing our core values with our employees. We aim to translate values into daily activities and leadership by translating values into actionable team directives.
